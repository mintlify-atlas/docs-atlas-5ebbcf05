## Biome categories

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Forest biomes" icon="tree">
    Dense woodlands featuring unique tree species including maple, magnolia, willow, and silver birch. Ranges from temperate groves to autumnal forests with distinctive foliage colors.
    
    **10 biomes total** including Autumnal Maple Forest, Bamboo Forest, Deciduous Forest, Magnolia Woodland, Maple Forest, Mauve Hills, Orchard, Silver Birch Forest, Temperate Grove, and Willow Forest.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Taiga biomes" icon="trees">
    Cold coniferous forests dominated by pine, redwood, and blackwood trees. Features both standard and sparse variants with unique tree arrangements.
    
    **6 biomes total** including Blackwood Taiga, Boreal Taiga, Golden Boreal Taiga, Pine Taiga, Redwoods, and Sparse Redwoods.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Plains biomes" icon="wheat">
    Open grasslands and fields with varied vegetation from flowers to crops. Includes meadows, prairies, and specialized field biomes.
    
    **9 biomes total** including Barley Fields, Clover Plains, Flower Fields, Grassland, Poppy Fields, Prairie, Pumpkin Fields, Rocky Meadow, and Shrubland.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Wetland biomes" icon="water">
    Swampy areas and rainforests with high moisture levels. Features bayous, fens, marshes, and tropical rainforests with dense vegetation.
    
    **8 biomes total** including Bayou, Eucalyptus Forest, Fen, Fungal Fen, Marsh, Old Growth Bayou, Rainforest, and Sparse Rainforest.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Arid biomes" icon="sun">
    Dry regions including deserts, savannas, and bushlands. Features unique desert vegetation like joshua trees, saguaro cacti, and baobabs.
    
    **6 biomes total** including Baobab Savanna, Dry Bushland, Joshua Desert, Outback, Saguaro Desert, and Steppe.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Mountain biomes" icon="mountain">
    High-altitude terrain with dramatic cliffs and peaks. Ranges from pine-covered slopes to arid mountains and towering formations.
    
    **5 biomes total** including Arid Mountains, Highland Fields, Mountains, Pine Slopes, and Towering Cliffs.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Coastal biomes" icon="water">
    Shoreline areas where land meets water. Features unique cliff formations and beach variants with different materials.
    
    **3 biomes total** including Chalk Cliffs, Grassy Beach, and Gravel Beach.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Aquatic biomes" icon="fish">
    Water-based biomes including rivers, underwater groves, and tropical waters. Features diverse aquatic vegetation and unique underwater terrain.
    
    **8 biomes total** including Alpha Grove, Ashen Woodland, Cold River, Hyacinth Deeps, Muddy River, Rocky Reef, Tropical River, and Tropics.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Frozen biomes" icon="snowflake">
    Cold snowy regions with ice formations. Includes frozen taigas, tundra, and dramatic ice spires reaching toward the sky.
    
    **6 biomes total** including Cold Boreal Taiga, Cold Deciduous Forest, Frozen Pine Taiga, Frozen Tundra, Icy Heights, and Spires.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Cave biomes" icon="gem">
    Underground ecosystems with unique flora and geology. Features glowing plants, massive mushrooms, crystal formations, and volcanic activity.
    
    **5 biomes total** including Ancient Delta, Bioshroom Caves, Prismachasm, Redstone Caves, and Scorching Caves.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Nether biomes" icon="fire-flame-curved">
    Hostile environments in the Nether dimension. Ranges from dark basalt basins to glowing meadows and toxic fungal undergrowth.
    
    **5 biomes total** including Blackstone Basin, Glistering Meadow, Infernal Holt, Mycotoxic Undergrowth, and Redstone Abyss.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Villager types

Biomes in Regions Unexplored support vanilla villager types based on their climate and terrain:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Plains villagers">
    Most temperate forest, plains, and coastal biomes feature Plains-type villagers, making them ideal for establishing settlements.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Taiga villagers">
    Cold forests, mountain regions, and northern biomes spawn Taiga-type villagers adapted to colder climates.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Desert villagers">
    Arid biomes like Joshua Desert and Saguaro Desert feature Desert-type villagers suited to harsh, dry conditions.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Swamp villagers">
    Wetland biomes including bayous, fens, and marshes spawn Swamp-type villagers.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Jungle villagers">
    Tropical and rainforest biomes feature Jungle-type villagers, including aquatic tropical areas.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Savanna villagers">
    Hot, dry biomes like Baobab Savanna and Outback spawn Savanna-type villagers.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Snow villagers">
    All frozen biomes feature Snow-type villagers adapted to extreme cold.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>
